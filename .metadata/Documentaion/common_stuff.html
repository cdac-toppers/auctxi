Frontend Architecture · Design System

A design system, not a page.

The difference between a beginner dashboard and a professional one isn't prettier pages—it's consistency. Don't just copy a template. Borrow its design language, build 20-30 reusable LEGO blocks, and assemble a product that feels purpose-built for AuctXI.

Section 01

Decide your visual language.

Before opening Figma or VS Code, lock in these rules. Don't introduce random colors, mixed fonts, or arbitrary spacing later. Consistency is what makes a UI look polished.

Section 03

Decide your navigation.

Don't just list pages. Think in a hierarchy. Group related functionality together so the sidebar isn't a mess of 15 flat links.

Dashboard Auction Management ├── Auctions └── Live Auction Team Management ├── Teams └── Players User Management └── Users Finance └── Payments Analytics └── Reports System ├── Notifications └── Settings
Section 04

Decide what NOT to build.

This is where you save days. Scope creep kills momentum. Shipped is better than perfect.

Do NOT build — Fancy animations, complex charts, resizable tables, drag and drop, column customization, dark mode, theme switcher, real notifications, real search, or real export. All these can come later.

Section 05

The build order.

Follow this exact sequence. Building the foundation first means you aren't reinventing the button on the Payments page.

Section 06

The AuctXI twist.

The inspiration is very CRUD-heavy (Cards, Filters, Table). That gets repetitive. Make AuctXI feel like an auction platform by adding small contextual panels.

Dashboard
  • "Current Live Auction" panel
  • Live countdown timer
  • Current highest bid display
Auctions
  • Progress indicator
  • Draft → Registration → Live → Completed
Teams
  • Budget remaining meter
  • Players acquired count
Players
  • Base price vs current bid
  • Sold / Unsold status
  • Clear visual emphasis

Domain-Specific — These kinds of elements make the product feel purpose-built rather than like a generic admin template.

Section 07

The 2-week roadmap.

Only after all pages are visually complete should you start replacing mock data with backend APIs. That separation keeps the frontend moving quickly and makes integration much smoother later.

Week 1
Foundation
  1. Design tokens (colors, typography, spacing)
  2. Layout (Sidebar, Header, Page Layout)
  3. Reusable UI components
  4. Mock data structure
Week 2
Pages
  1. Dashboard
  2. Auctions
  3. Teams
  4. Players
  5. Users
  6. Payments
  7. Reports
  8. Settings
